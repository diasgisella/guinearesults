Section B: Data on the Farming and Trade of wild mammals and birds

2 activities reported. One from EBO SURSY and One from the Ministry of the Environment:

  • EBO SURSY collected data about live animals and meat or other products consumed as food, specifically regarding mammals, to gather all parameters on domestic and wild animals at borders and at livestock and poultry markets. The data was collected regarding price, demand, consumer characterization, and wildlife markets, in a sub national level. Data is shared with government entities, in a One healh approach, by administrative means, when there is data to share. These information are used to facilitate decision-making.

  • The Ministry of the Environment collected data specifically about primates, Cephalophus, and Potamochoerus. Regarding the trade of Ornamental products (e.g.ivory, feathers, shells). The data was also part of a value chain studies, that was done in the prectures (districts) of mamou (myenne guinea), macenta (forest guinea), kankan, siguiri and mandiana (upper guinea). Aimed at changing social behavior and reducing transmission of zoonotic diseases. Data were collected in a subnational level, and shared with other government entities (Ministries of Health and Livestock, and the OH platform), through reports, at a regular basis. Data is used in areas of awareness-raising campaigns.

Section C: Data on distribution and movement of wild mammals and birds

3 activities reported in the section:

  • EBO SURSY collected data from animals in Rehabilitation center and Natural habitat, regarding animal movements, demographic data and Community wildlife management, with the goal of collecting health information. Data are collected at Community level and reported to the sub-prefecture, prefecture and central level and shared with OH platform By administrative means and internet when there is data available.

  • Ministry of the Environment collected data about Phacochoerus from Natural Habitat, Parks and around Protected Areas, regarding the dstribution of suitable habitats for certain animals and Community Reserves. Data are collected at Sub-national, and used by the actors in awareness-raising, capacity building and cross-borde. Data is shared through reports regularly (Weekly)

  • Guine Ecologie collected data about Pan troglodytes verus from its Natural habitat, with the goal of identifying viable habitats for chimpanzees and understanding the Cohabitation between chimpanzees and local communities. Data is collected at a Sub-national level, specifically from the prefectures of Gaoual, Koundara, Kindia, Telimele, Dabola and Dinguiraye. Data is used to contribute for the conservation of chimpanzees, and shared with the Ministry of Environment, Water and Forestry ad hoc, in meetings and workshops.

Section D: Data on the health of wild mammals and birds

3 activities reported:

  • EBO SURSY collected data on Live animals crossing borders and customs posts. They dont collect data on a specific disease. The analysis of the samples collected were performed through Serological tests and Pathogen detection, and done on National Laboratories of the Ministry of Livestock/Agriculture (LCVD and regional laboratories). The data is used to develop a TOR and its budget for a vaccination program and risk-based surveillance (active surveillance).

  • Centre de Recherche et de Formation en Infectiologie de Guine (CERFIG) collected data on Free roaming bats, for specific diseases (Viral hemorrhagic fevers of Ebola, Marburg, rift valley, Crim-Congo and Lassa). Diagnostics tests were done in the California Laboratory. Data of this activity is used to raise awareness among communities about zoonotic diseases and Strengthen surveillance protocols.

  • Ministry of the Environment collected data on Animal products (Smoked meat) of Bats, to study Ebola virus hemorrhagic fever and Lassa fever, through the collection of blood samples, that were analysed through PCR tests. The data was used to Guide risk monitoring, and were shared internally (within the ministry of environment), with the Ministry of Elevation, and on the One Health Platform, through emails and newsletters, in a regular basis (weekly).

Section E - Outbreak investigation involving wildlife

4 activities reported:

  • PREDICT participated in 2 Ebola virus disease outbreak investigations, through the Ministry of Livestock/Agriculture, the Ministry of Environment and the ministry of Health. Data were collected on Bats, Rodents, Small ruminants and Pigs, and the samples collected were analysed using PCR and ELISA, in the UC-Davis One Health and Veterinary Medicine Institute Laboratory. Data was shared with The National Agency for Health Security and the Ministry of Health through reports.

  • EBO SURSY participated in Ebola Virus disease and Avian influenza outbreak investigations, through the Ministry of Livestock/Agriculture. Data were collected on mammals and birds crossing border posts/customs and the samples collected were analysed using PCR and ELISA, in LCVD and LNFH. Data was shared at the OH platform at a ad hoc basis.

  • Centre de Recherche et de Formation en Infectiologie de Guine (CERFIG) participated in 4 outbreak investigations about Ebola virus disease and Marburg Fever, in collaboration with the National Directorate of Veteran Services and with Environmental Services. They collected data on Free-roaming and farmed animals, specifically Bats and rodents (and bushmeat).

  • Ministry of the Environment participated in 3 investigations (Anthrax, Lassa Fever, Ebola Hemorrhagic Fever), through the ministry of Health, in collaboration with The Community, the One Health Platform and the Lassa Fever Project. Data were collected in Mice, bats,chimpanzees, Cephalophus spp, kept in zoos. Samples were analysed in the Laboratoire Fivre Hmoragique Nongo Conakry, the Laboratoire Central Vtrinaire Diagnostic and the Laboratory of california. Data is shared with the Ministry of livestock (National Veterinary Service), Ministry of Health (ANSS), Ministry of the Environment (OGPR) and Guinean Office of Parksand Reserves through emails in a regular basis.
